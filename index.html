ABOUT US

The benefits of art, both producing and experiencing it, are well known.
For one, producing art is a form of self-expression. Artists can say with their art what they cannot say with their words. Psychologically, it is proven that art, in any form, can help relieve stress and increase positive emotions. Despite all these positives, people rarely make time to indulge in creating art.
Graphite Stories is an organisation that finds ways to make art accessible to people.
B.L Kavyashree, the founder of Graphite Stories is an artist herself, having painted and drawn her way through her adolescent years. Initially, she started Graphite Stories as an Instagram page where she could showcase and sell her art, and take orders on commission. Eventually however, Kavya realised that there was more she could do with the platform. 
While still in college, she and her friend, Sharan started approaching schools and colleges in the name of Graphite stories, looking for opportunities to conduct workshops for students that not only encourage creativity, but also stimulate artistic expression among individuals.
In addition to various projects, Graphite Stories also conduct art competitions to help raise awareness about pressing issues. With backing from multiple corporates, the organisation provides art supplies required for competitors, allowing those who cannot afford to attend such competitions the opportunity to do so.

EVENTS

Project Smiles, St. Patrick’s Boys High School, Bangalore
Graphite stories founder, Kavya conducted an art workshop in association with St. Joseph’s College of Commerce throughout the month of December for 8th and 9th grade students on the basics of pencil shading and drawing techniques. The hour-long workshop conducted everyday, provided insights ranging from basic anatomy sketches to lighting and perspective shading techniques. The workshop concluded with an art competition in which the students were graded on how well they had applied all that they had leant during the sessions conducted.
L&W Construction Pvt Ltd- Family Day
Graphite Stories conducted a one-day workshop at L&W Construction Pvt Ltd’s family day held at White Feather, Electronic City. Two workshops namely, origami for kids and paper bag making were conducted simultaneously to cater to the plethora of age groups that were present on that day. Origami for kids included paper swan, variety of paper flowers, ninja star and so on. The paper bag-making workshop was divided into two sessions; one was a painting session that was done in association with YouVa. It is a stationery brand of Navneet designed keeping the youth of India in mind. The other session was the paper bag making session to encourage citizens to move from plastic covers to paper bags.
Beautifying Bangalore
It is an ongoing project where certain prime locations are chosen and the walls of which are painted in order to beautify Bangalore. The latest being, Nrupatunga Road Underpass in front of St. Martha’s Hospital, that was infested with unwanted posters and notices. The wall was cleaned and beautifully painted by young artists, one of them done by Graphite Stories. This was as part of a CSR project of MyPeriodStory.com to addresses the universal stigma about menstruation. Note: The painting above was only a part of the entire painting project.
